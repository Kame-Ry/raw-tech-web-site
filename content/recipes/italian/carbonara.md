**Easy | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 15 min | Serves: 2**

## Ingredients

- 200g spaghetti  
- 100g pancetta (or Bacon)  
- 2 large eggs  
- 50g pecorino cheese, finely grated  
- 50g parmesan, finely grated  
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled and left whole  
- 50g unsalted butter  
- Freshly ground black pepper  
- Sea salt

## Method

1. **Prep the pasta**  
   Cook the spaghetti in a large pan of salted boiling water until al dente. (8 Minutes)

2. **Prepare the sauce**  
   Beat the eggs in a medium bowl, season with black pepper, and stir in the cheeses.

3. **Cook the pancetta**  
   Meanwhile, melt the butter in a frying pan. Add the pancetta and garlic and fry until crisp. Discard garlic once golden.

4. **Combine**  
   Drain the pasta, reserving some cooking water. Add pasta to the pan with pancetta, remove from heat.

5. **Add egg mixture**  
   Quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture. Toss vigorously, adding a little pasta water to create a silky sauce.

6. **Serve**  
   Top with extra cheese and freshly cracked pepper. Serve immediately.

## Cook With Your Senses

> *Inspired by [Ethan Chlebowski's](https://www.ethanchlebowski.com/) sensory approach to cooking — the idea that your senses should tell you more than a timer ever could.*

- **Look:** The pancetta should be deeply golden, not just cooked — that colour is flavour. The final sauce should be glossy and coat the pasta like silk, not sit in a puddle at the bottom of the bowl.
- **Listen:** When the pancetta hits the pan, you want a steady sizzle, not a violent crack. If it's spitting aggressively, the heat's too high.
- **Smell:** When the garlic goes golden and the pancetta fat renders, the kitchen should smell like the best Italian restaurant you've ever been in. That's your cue.
- **Touch:** The pasta should have resistance when you bite it — not crunchy, not soft. Al dente is a texture, not a time.
- **Taste:** Before you serve, taste the sauce. It should be salty from the cheese and pork, rich from the egg, and sharp from the pepper. If it's flat, it needs more pepper or a pinch of salt.

## Notes

- Work fast with the egg mix to avoid scrambling.  
- Use guanciale instead of pancetta for a traditional version.  
- Don’t add cream, real carbonara doesn’t need it.
